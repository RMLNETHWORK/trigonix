📐 Trigonometry in Surveying

Modern surveying relies on trigonometric calculations to map terrain and establish precise boundaries.

Key Instruments

  • Theodolites: Measure horizontal/vertical angles
  • Total Stations: Combine angle and distance measurement
  • GPS Receivers: Use trilateration principles

Common Calculations

Height Difference: Δh = d·sinθ + i - r
Where:
d = slope distance
θ = vertical angle
i = instrument height
r = rod reading

Topographic Survey

Measuring hill elevation:
Slope distance = 150m
Vertical angle = 12°
Δh = 150sin(12°) ≈ 31.2m
